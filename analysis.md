3. Why does traffic to Lagos route through Europe?

Traffic to Lagos (Nigeria) often routes through Europe due to historical and infrastructural reasons. Much of Africa’s internet connectivity was originally built to connect to European networks rather than directly to other regions.
Submarine cables such as the Africa Coast to Europe (ACE) cable system connect West Africa to Europe, making Europe a central hub for routing traffic.
As a result, data from other continents is often sent to Europe first and then routed back down to Africa. This indirect routing increases latency and contributes to higher inefficiency ratios.

What would need to change?
To reduce this inefficiency, the network would need more direct submarine cables connecting Africa to other continents (e.g., Africa ↔ Americas, Africa ↔ Asia),
improved intra-African connectivity between countries, and more local internet exchange points (IXPs) to keep traffic within the region.
New projects like the 2Africa cable system are helping improve connectivity and reduce reliance on European routing hubs.
